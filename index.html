Scoring Methodology

Hot 70 – 100

Strong bidirectional engagement, recent contact, likely decision-maker. These contacts are actively warm and most likely to respond to outreach.

Warm 50 – 69

Good back-and-forth messaging, moderate recency. Worth re-engaging with a personalized message referencing your past conversation.

Cool 30 – 49

Some engagement but less frequent or older. May need a warm-up message before pitching. Still worth including in drip campaigns.

Score Components

Engagement0 – 40 ptsMessage volume, back-and-forth balance, conversation depth. Bidirectional exchanges score highest.
Recency0 – 25 ptsHow recently you exchanged messages. Last 30 days = full points; older = fewer points.
Title0 – 20 ptsDecision-maker seniority (C-suite, VP, Director, etc.). Requires Connections CSV for accurate data.
Relevance0 – 15 ptsKeywords in conversations: AI, automation, operating models, digital transformation, consulting.

Spam Filtering

One-way inbound messages (cold outreach you never replied to) receive heavy score penalties and are effectively filtered out. "LinkedIn Member" accounts are excluded entirely.

Search Tips

The search bar searches across names, titles, companies, and message content. You can also use prefixes:

  • title:VP — filter by job title
  • company:EY — filter by company
  • mentioned:AI — search message content
  • tag:priority — filter by your custom tags
  • Natural language: "Who mentioned automation?"
